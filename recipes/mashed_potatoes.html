Restaurant-Style Mashed Potatoes

Description

Don't mashed-potatoes just hit different at restaurants? Perhaps the chef at Olive Garden carries a certain genius that can not be copied, or perhaps most restaurants don't serve the typical creamy mashed potatoes, but rather something quite different. Hence, we will be making garlic restaurant-style mashed potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs red potatoes or 8 small red potatoes, quartered, skin left on
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, divided
  • 1/4 teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesean cheese
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, cut into pieces

Steps

  1. Place potatoes in a large pot or dutch oven.
  2. Add enough water to cover the potatoes.
  3. Stir in 1 teaspoon of Kosher salt.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover and simmer about 20 to 25 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, place the remaining 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, pepper, garlic powder, sour cream, Parmesan cheese and butter in the bowl of an electric mixer.
  6. When potatoes pierce easily with a paring knife, remove from heat and drain.
  7. Add potatoes to the bowl to the bowl with the other ingredients and mix on medium speed untill mostly smooth.
  8. Serve.
